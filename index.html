The outcome is sustained consideration and brand recall in the Nordic premium-pool market, not a single spike that fades.

Always-on · Two seasons · Brand recall

The scale of it

A pool like this isn't added to a home — it's what the home is built around. A complete Compass Pools flagship: the ~3×6–4×8 m one-piece pool — self-cleaning, with underwater lighting and cover, delivered to Sweden and built to last decades. A build of this class runs SEK 450,000–700,000 (≈ €40,000–61,000) — the kind of thing a family plans a whole garden around. What Villa Sjövik brings back isn't counted the same way: two years inside that home, permanent rights to every frame, and the trust of 194,000 people who are deciding where their own pool will go.

Each side brings what the other can't buy — a flagship pool, and two years inside a home that 194,000 people trust.

The journey

From bare slab to finished oasis.

  1. 01

    Design

    The vision set together — placement beneath the deck, the above-ground line of the pool, the finish and the space around it. Documented from the very first sketch.

  2. 02

    Delivery

    The pool arrives in Sweden as a single finished unit. Villa Sjövik pours the concrete slab and prepares the site — no traditional excavation, which is the whole point of the above-ground setup.

  3. 03

    Installation

    Compass Pools sets the pool on the slab; Villa Sjövik builds the wooden deck out around it — the Scandinavian showcase for a premium pool on sloped, challenging terrain.

  4. 04

    24 months of content

    Around 18 films and 350+ images across two summers — from the build to a flagship master edit, then seasonal hero features, ongoing organic presence and the permanent SEO articles. The full campaign calendar.
